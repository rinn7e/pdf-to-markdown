# Law
## On
### The Organization of Courts

[IMAGE: Seal of the Ministry of Royal Palace]

# Chapter 1
# General Provisions

**Article 1.-**
This Law aims to:
- Guarantee the independence of the judicial power (អំណាចតុលាការ)
- Ensure impartiality (អនាគតិ) and protect the rights and freedoms of citizens
- Ensure the smooth functioning of judicial bodies (អង្គការតុលាការ) and prosecution bodies (អង្គការអយ្យការ)
- Enhance public services for efficiency and speed
- Ensure justice for all types of cases to increase public trust and contribute to strengthening social safety.

**Article 2.-**
The goals of this Law are to:
- Define the organization and functioning of judicial bodies of all types and levels, which are the Courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តី) of the Kingdom of Cambodia
- Define the organization and functioning of prosecution bodies attached to the Courts of all levels in the Kingdom of Cambodia
- Define the jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច) of Courts of all types and levels according to their specialization
- Organize the management, administration, and functioning of the Courts.

**Article 3.-**
The Courts of the Kingdom of Cambodia include:
- First Instance Courts (សាលាដំបូង)
- Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍)
- Supreme Court (តុលាការកំពូល).

The First Instance Court is a lower court (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ទាប). The Appeal Court and the Supreme Court are higher courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ខ្ពស់).

Based on actual necessity, the establishment of any other type of Court or specialized Court (សាលាជម្រះក្តីឯកទេស) shall be done by law.

**Article 4.-**
Courts of all types and levels exercise judicial power that covers all cases, including administrative cases.

**Article 5.-**
Lower courts shall be divided into specialized courts (តុលាការជំនាញ). The establishment, abolition, and modification of specialized courts shall be defined by Royal Decree (ព្រះរាជក្រឹត្យ).

The President of the First Instance Court has direct administrative hierarchy over all presidents of specialized courts of that First Instance Court. Each president of a specialized court has direct administrative hierarchy over the judges who are members. The President of the First Instance Court shall not serve as the president of any specialized court. Each president of a specialized court can only be the president of one specialized court in that First Instance Court.

Higher courts shall be divided into specialized chambers (សភាជំនាញ). The establishment, abolition, and modification of specialized chambers shall be defined by Royal Decree.

The President of a higher court has direct administrative hierarchy over all presidents of specialized chambers of that higher court. Each president of a specialized chamber has direct administrative hierarchy over the judges who are members. The President of a higher court shall not serve as the president of any specialized chamber. Each president of a specialized chamber can only be the president of one specialized chamber in that higher court.

**Article 6.-**
Only judges (ចៅក្រម) have the right to adjudicate. Judges shall perform this duty independently, strictly adhering to the law and with all their heart and conscience (សម្បជញ្ញៈ).

Adjudication for justice shall be done in the name of the Khmer people according to the procedures and laws in force.

Only Courts have the power to adjudicate and issue court decisions (សេចក្តីសម្រេចរបស់តុលាការ).

**Article 7.-**
Hearings (សវនាការ) shall be public, unless such publicity could endanger public order or good customs, or if there are specific provisions in the law stating otherwise.

In all cases, judgments (សាលក្រម) and final judgments (សាលដីកា) of the court shall be announced in a public hearing.

Deliberations for court decisions by the trial panel (អង្គជំនុំជម្រះ) shall be confidential.

Unless otherwise provided, the announcement of court judgments or final judgments shall be made by the presiding judge (ប្រធានអង្គជំនុំជម្រះ) or one of the judges who is a member of the trial panel.

Every court judgment or final judgment must have grounds (សំអាងហេតុ).

Unless otherwise provided, judgments and final judgments must be signed by the presiding judge and countersigned by the court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

**Article 8.-**
Attached to each First Instance Court, there shall be a prosecution body called the Prosecution Office (អយ្យការ) attached to the First Instance Court.

Attached to each Appeal Court, there shall be a prosecution body called the General Prosecution Office (មហាអយ្យការ) attached to the Appeal Court.

Attached to the Supreme Court, there shall be a prosecution body called the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court.

A representative of the prosecution office must be present at all hearings of criminal cases and other cases as defined by law.

**Article 9.-**
In every judicial body and prosecution body of all levels, there are clerks (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) and administrative officers, and there may be legal technical officers (មន្ត្រីជំនាញផ្នែកច្បាប់) as assistants as necessary.

Clerks assist judges and prosecutors at all stages of court and prosecution procedures.

The role and functions of clerks shall be defined in the Statute of Clerks (លក្ខន្តិកៈក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The modalities and conditions for selecting legal technical officers shall be defined by Sub-decree (អនុក្រឹត្យ).

**Article 10.-**
In each Court of all levels, there is an administrative unit which is under the central administration of the Ministry of Justice to support the functioning of the judicial and prosecution bodies.

At the First Instance Court level, this administrative unit is called the Administrative Secretariat (លេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាល) of the First Instance Court, having a rank equivalent to a department.

At the Appeal Court level, this administrative unit is called the General Secretariat of Administration (អគ្គលេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាល) of the Appeal Court, having a rank equivalent to a general department.

At the Supreme Court level, this administrative unit is called the General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court, having a rank equivalent to a general department.

**Article 11.-**
The Ministry of Justice has the right to inspect the administrative affairs of all Courts, with a General Department of Court Administrative Affairs as the technical body. The Ministry of Justice may issue legal instruments and necessary instructions to ensure the smooth functioning of court administration management at all levels.

In necessary cases, the Minister of Justice may assign an inspection (អធិការកិច្ច) on any specific issue and then report it to the Supreme Council of the Magistracy (ឧត្តមក្រុមប្រឹក្សានៃអង្គចៅក្រម) for review and decision.

Higher courts have the jurisdiction to review the adjudication work of lower courts. This review takes place within the framework of appeal procedures in accordance with the provisions on procedures in force.

# Chapter 2
# First Instance Courts and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court

## Section 1
## First Instance Court

### Sub-section 1
### Organization and Functioning of the First Instance Court

**Article 12.-**
The First Instance Court is the court of first instance, which shall be established in Phnom Penh and in every province of the Kingdom of Cambodia. The operationalization of these First Instance Courts shall be done by Royal Decree.

The First Instance Court shall be divided into specialized courts. The First Instance Court is a court of general jurisdiction (សាលាជម្រះក្តីទូទៅ) which has the jurisdiction to adjudicate all cases, except for cases that fall under the jurisdiction of specialized courts or extraordinary courts established by separate laws.

The composition of the First Instance Court includes:
- President
- Vice-president
- Judges.

**Article 13.-**
The personnel of the First Instance Court includes:
- President
- Vice-president
- Judges
- Clerks
- Administrative officers and may include other legal technical officers who work at the First Instance Court.

**Article 14.-**
Each First Instance Court is divided into specialized courts as follows:
- Civil Court (តុលាការរដ្ឋប្បវេណី)
- Criminal Court (តុលាការព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ)
- Commercial Court (តុលាការពាណិជ្ជកម្ម)
- Labour Court (តុលាការការងារ).

Other specialized courts of the First Instance Court may be established according to necessary needs. This establishment shall be done by Royal Decree.

Each specialized court of the First Instance Court issues its decisions autonomously within its jurisdiction in the name of the First Instance Court where that specialized court is located.

**Article 15.-**
The composition of each specialized court includes:
- Court President
- Judges
- Clerks.

The president of each specialized court, as well as judges and clerks, shall be assigned by the President of the First Instance Court. The process of these specialized courts shall be defined by the President of the First Instance Court in accordance with the legal instruments in force.

**Article 16.-**
Each specialized court of the First Instance Court adjudicates with a single judge (ចៅក្រមទោល) or by a trial panel (ក្រុមប្រឹក្សាជំនុំជម្រះ) in accordance with the provisions on civil procedure, criminal procedure, or other procedural provisions in force.

In case any judge is busy or unable to perform duties, that judge may be replaced by a judge in the subsequent order as specified in the annual work allocation table of the President of the First Instance Court.

In cases where any specialized court cannot hold a hearing due to a lack of judges, the President of the First Instance Court shall assign judges from other specialized courts to fill the position. If it is necessary to request one or more judges from another First Instance Court to fill the composition of this specialized court, the President of the First Instance Court shall request the Minister of Justice.

**Article 17.-**
The President of the First Instance Court takes necessary management measures for the functioning of the First Instance Court. In case the President of the First Instance Court is unable to work or if the position of the President of the First Instance Court is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall assign a vice-president of the First Instance Court or, if there is no vice-president, assign any judge to serve as the acting president to ensure the functioning of the First Instance Court until the official appointment of a President of the First Instance Court. The appointment of the President of the First Instance Court shall be made in the shortest period and at most not exceeding 30 (thirty) days.

The President of the First Instance Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Assign any vice-president of the First Instance Court to manage tasks on his behalf in necessary cases.
- Assign vice-presidents of the First Instance Court to be in charge as presidents of each specialized court based on their expertise or work experience. In case of a lack of vice-presidents, the President of the First Instance Court may assign any judge who has a high rank or much experience to serve as the president of a specialized court.
- Define the order to replace judges in each specialized court and in the trial panels of these specialized courts in case any judge is absent or busy.
- Assign and transfer clerks within each specialized court according to actual necessity.

**Article 18.-**
In case of necessity, the Minister of Justice may decide to allow the First Instance Court to hold hearings outside its office location upon the request of the President of the First Instance Court.

### Sub-section 2
### Jurisdiction

**Article 19.-**
The First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases that the law entrusts the jurisdiction to other courts.

In necessary cases to ensure public interest, safety, social security, and the smooth functioning of judicial justice, the Minister of Justice may decide to transfer the jurisdiction of handling a case from one First Instance Court to another.

In case of jurisdictional disputes (វិវាទសមត្ថកិច្ច) between different specialized courts within a First Instance Court, the President of the First Instance Court shall convene a meeting of the presidents of the involved specialized courts at his discretion or upon the request of any specialized court president to decide on the jurisdiction. The President of the First Instance Court decides on this jurisdictional dispute by a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច). This decision is final and without appeal.

**Article 20.-**
The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates all criminal cases within its jurisdiction by applying the provisions on criminal procedure, unless otherwise provided by law.

The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates criminal cases with a single judge or by a trial panel in accordance with the provisions on criminal procedure.

The Investigating Chamber (មន្ទីរស៊ើបសួរ) of the First Instance Court is a part of the Criminal Court and has the jurisdiction to investigate criminal cases by applying the provisions on criminal procedure. The President of the First Instance Court shall assign at least 02 (two) judges by a decision to perform the duties of investigating judges (ចៅក្រមស៊ើបសួរ) at the Investigating Chamber of the Criminal Court of the First Instance Court. This decision shall also define the numerical order for receiving and performing tasks. In case there is no investigating judge or a lack of investigating judges, the President of the First Instance Court may assign judges from other specialized courts to perform investigative functions.

In case of necessity, the investigating judge may perform duties as a judge at other specialized courts as assigned by the President of the First Instance Court.

**Article 21.-**
The Civil Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate civil cases and any cases where there is no provision entrusting jurisdiction to other specialized courts by applying the provisions on civil procedure.

The Civil Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates civil cases with a single judge or by a trial panel by applying the provisions on civil procedure.

**Article 22.-**
The Commercial Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate commercial cases, including bankruptcy in commercial matters, by applying the provisions on commercial procedure.

**Article 23.-**
The Commercial Court adjudicates with 03 (three) judges and is assisted by 02 (two) advisors (ទីប្រឹក្សា) who are merchants or people with knowledge of commercial law in case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value of 1,000,000,000 (one billion) Riels or more.

The Commercial Court adjudicates with 01 (one) judge and is assisted by 02 (two) advisors who are merchants or people with knowledge of commercial law in case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value from 100,000,000 (one hundred million) Riels to less than 1,000,000,000 (one billion) Riels.

The Commercial Court adjudicates with 01 (one) judge without the need for the participation of commercial advisors in case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value of less than 100,000,000 (one hundred million) Riels.

The judgment of the Commercial Court shall be decided by 01 (one) judge or 03 (three) judges according to the 1st and 2nd paragraphs above after consultation with the commercial advisors.

The commercial advisors do not perform their functions permanently in the Commercial Court of the First Instance Court. Commercial advisors shall come to perform their functions at the First Instance Court upon the invitation of the President of the Commercial Court of the First Instance Court.

**Article 24.-**
The modalities for appointing and performing the duties of commercial advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the Minister of Commerce (រដ្ឋមន្ត្រីក្រសួងពាណិជ្ជកម្ម).

**Article 25.-**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate labour cases by applying the provisions on labour procedure.

**Article 26.-**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall adjudicate with 01 (one) judge assisted by 02 (two) labour advisors (ទីប្រឹក្សាការងារ), where 01 (one) is an employee (កម្មករនិយោជិត) and the other 01 (one) is an employer (និយោជក).

The judgment of the Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall be decided by 01 (one) judge after consultation with the labour advisors.

The labour advisors do not perform their functions permanently in the Labour Court of the First Instance Court. Labour advisors shall participate in performing their functions at the First Instance Court upon the invitation of the President of the Labour Court.

**Article 27.-**
The Labour Court with jurisdiction is the Labour Court of the First Instance Court at the place of the labour dispute.

Despite the jurisdictional determination as in the above paragraph, employees may file a lawsuit to the First Instance Court as defined below, unless otherwise provided:
- At their residence, or
- At the location of the head office or principal place of business of the company, or
- At the residence of the representative or person in charge of the company if there is no head office or principal place of business of the company.

**Article 28.-**
The modalities for electing and performing the duties of labour advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the Minister in charge of the labour sector (រដ្ឋមន្ត្រីក្រសួងទទួលបន្ទុកវិស័យការងារ).

## Section 2
## Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court

**Article 29.-**
The composition of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court includes:
- Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Clerks
- Administrative officers and may include other legal technical officers who work at the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

**Article 30.-**
The prosecution body is an indivisible body (អង្គអវិភាគ). The Prosecution Office has the function to file a public action (បណ្តឹងអាជ្ញា) and request the implementation of the law before the investigating jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការស៊ើបសួរ) and trial jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការជំនុំជម្រះ), as well as perform other functions according to the determination of the law and regulations in force.

The Prosecutor and Deputy Prosecutor are representatives of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court where they work. Deputy Prosecutors participate in implementing the functions of the Prosecution Office under the leadership and joint responsibility of the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

In case of being busy, sick, or absent, the Prosecutor shall assign any Deputy Prosecutor to replace him. In case the Prosecutor is unable to work or if the position of the Prosecutor is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall assign any Deputy Prosecutor to serve as the acting prosecutor to ensure the functioning of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court until the official appointment of a Prosecutor.

**Article 31.-**
The Prosecutor has power over all Deputy Prosecutors within his jurisdiction. The Prosecutor assigns and allocates work to all Deputy Prosecutors.

The Prosecutor has the right to issue orders to Deputy Prosecutors to carry out prosecutions or to assign prosecutions or to make any conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្និដ្ឋាន) as he deems appropriate. The conclusions of the Deputy Prosecutor must be sent to the Prosecutor for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor does not agree with those conclusions and the representative of the prosecution who made those conclusions still refuses to follow, the Prosecutor may assign other Deputy Prosecutors or assign himself to be the representative of the prosecution in the hearing of the First Instance Court.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor may make oral remarks (កត់សម្គាល់ដោយផ្ទាល់មាត់) freely as he deems in accordance with his conscience. No disciplinary charge can be made against the representative of the prosecution due to his oral remarks during the hearing being different from the written conclusions.

## Section 3
## Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court

**Article 32.-**
The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court is the technical body for the First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court on:
- Personnel, administrative, and financial work
- Management of court administration for technical assistance to the work programs of the First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court
- Preparation of budget projects for the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor for review and decision
- Reporting on personnel, administrative, and financial work regularly to the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor
- Implementing budget work according to the approved projects
- Public relations work
- Sending judgments of the First Instance Court to the Ministry of Justice regularly
- Preparing and sending monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, and annual personnel, administrative, and financial work reports to the Ministry of Justice
- Other tasks assigned by the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court
- Implementing other tasks as defined by legal instruments.

The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court shall be divided into offices (ការិយាល័យ) according to actual necessary numbers. Each office is led by a chief and a number of deputy chiefs as necessary.

The organization and functioning of the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice.

**Article 33.-**
The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court is led by a chief (ប្រធាន) and a number of deputy chiefs (អនុប្រធាន) as necessary. The Chief of the Secretariat shall be appointed by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

Officers working at the secretariat below the Chief of the Secretariat shall be appointed by a Prokas (បទបញ្ជា/ប្រកាស) of the Minister of Justice.

The Chief and Deputy Chief of the Secretariat shall be selected from civil servants who have experience working in legal, administrative, or financial fields for at least 05 (five) years. Office chiefs and deputy chiefs shall be selected from civil servants who have experience working in legal, administrative, or financial fields for at least 03 (three) years.

All officers working at the secretariat, including the Chief of the Secretariat, shall have their framework (ក្របខណ្ឌ) at the Ministry of Justice.

**Article 34.-**
The Assembly (សន្និបាត) of the First Instance Court shall take place under the presidency of the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court to summarize past work results and define directions for the administrative work of the First Instance Court for the following year. The report of the assembly of the First Instance Court shall be sent to the Ministry of Justice.

# Chapter 3
# Appeal Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court

## Section 1
## Appeal Court

### Sub-section 1
### Organization and Functioning of the Appeal Court

**Article 35.-**
The Appeal Court (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍) is the court of second instance.

The Appeal Court includes the Phnom Penh Appeal Court and Regional Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍តំបន់). The operationalization of each Regional Appeal Court and the determination of its territorial jurisdiction shall be done by Royal Decree.

**Article 36.-**
The composition of the Appeal Court includes:
- President
- Vice-president
- Judges
- Clerks and administrative officers, and may include other legal technical officers who work at the Appeal Court.

**Article 37.-**
The Appeal Court has specialized chambers (សភាជំនាញ) as follows:
- Criminal Chamber (សភាព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ)
- Civil Chamber (សភារដ្ឋប្បវេណី)
- Investigating Chamber (សភាស៊ើបសួរ)
- Commercial Chamber (សភាពាណិជ្ជកម្ម)
- Labour Chamber (សភាការងារ).

In case of necessity, other specialized chambers of the Appeal Court may be established by Royal Decree.

Each chamber issues its decisions autonomously within its jurisdiction in the name of the Appeal Court where that chamber is located.

**Article 38.-**
The composition of each chamber includes:
- Chamber President
- Judges and
- Clerks.

The president of each chamber shall be assigned by the President of the Appeal Court. The number of judges, clerks, and the process of these chambers shall be defined by the President of the Appeal Court.

**Article 39.-**
Each chamber of the Appeal Court adjudicates with 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, in accordance with the procedural provisions in force.

Any case that has been returned by the Supreme Court shall be adjudicated by the Appeal Court in a hearing with 05 (five) judges, all of whom are judges who have not participated in the adjudication of that case before, by applying the procedural provisions in force.

**Article 40.-**
The President of the Appeal Court takes necessary management measures for the functioning of the Appeal Court.

The President of the Appeal Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Assign any vice-president of the Appeal Court to manage tasks on his behalf in necessary cases.
- Assign vice-presidents of the Appeal Court to be in charge as presidents of each chamber based on their expertise and work experience. In case of a lack of vice-presidents of the Appeal Court, the President of the Appeal Court may assign any judge who has a high rank or much work experience to serve as the chamber president.
- Define the order to replace judges in the trial panels of each chamber in case any judge is absent or busy.
- Assign and transfer clerks within each chamber as actually necessary.

### Sub-section 2
### Jurisdiction

**Article 41.-**
The Appeal Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases that the law entrusts the jurisdiction to other courts.

**Article 42.-**
The Appeal Court decides on both facts (អង្គហេតុ) and law (អង្គច្បាប់) on cases within its jurisdiction in accordance with the laws and regulations in force.

**Article 43.-**
The Criminal Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against the criminal judgments of the First Instance Court and other cases within its jurisdiction by applying the criminal procedure provisions in force.

**Article 44.-**
The Civil Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and protests (បណ្តឹងជំទាស់) against court decisions of the First Instance Court in civil cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by applying the civil procedure provisions in force.

The decisions shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president.

**Article 45.-**
The Investigating Chamber has the jurisdiction to handle complaints against investigation acts or court decisions of investigating judges and other cases within its jurisdiction by applying the criminal procedure provisions in force. The decisions of the Investigating Chamber shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president.

**Article 46.-**
The Commercial Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and protests (បណ្តឹងជំទាស់) against court decisions of the First Instance Court in commercial cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by applying the commercial procedure provisions. The Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court shall adjudicate with 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, and is assisted by 02 (two) advisors who are merchants or people with knowledge of commercial law and who were not commercial advisors in the same case that was adjudicated by the First Instance Court and the Appeal Court. The final judgment of the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, after consultation with the commercial advisors.

The commercial advisors do not perform their functions permanently in the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court. Commercial advisors shall come to perform their functions only upon the invitation of the President of the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court.

The modalities for appointing and performing the duties of commercial advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the Minister of Commerce.

**Article 47.-**
The Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and protests against court decisions of the First Instance Court in labour cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by applying the labour procedure provisions. The Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court shall adjudicate with 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, and is assisted by 02 (two) advisors, where 01 (one) is an employee and the other 01 (one) is an employer, and who were not labour advisors in the same case that was adjudicated by the First Instance Court and the Appeal Court.

The decisions of the Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, after consultation with the labour advisors.

The labour advisors do not perform their functions permanently in the Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court. Labour advisors shall come to perform their functions only upon the invitation of the President of the Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court.

The modalities for appointing and performing the duties of labour advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the Minister in charge of the labour sector.

**Article 48.-**
Joint Chambers (សភារួមក្រុម) may be established to operate by a decision of the President of the Appeal Court at his initiation or upon the request of any chamber president when any case raises an issue that falls under the jurisdiction of many chambers or if that issue has received conflicting solutions before different chambers of the same Appeal Court. The Joint Chambers shall function upon the invitation of the President of the Appeal Court.

The decisions of the Joint Chambers on jurisdiction between different chambers and legal disputes (វិវាទច្បាប់) have the power to force all chambers of the Appeal Court to comply.

When the Joint Chambers are established, this chamber must have at least 05 (five) judges who are equally divided between judges representing the involved chambers. The Joint Chambers are under the presidency of the President of the Appeal Court.

The President of the Appeal Court issues a decision in accordance with the decision of the Joint Chambers. Decisions on jurisdictional disputes and legal disputes are final and without appeal.

## Section 2
## General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court

**Article 49.-**
Attached to each Appeal Court, there is a General Prosecution Office (មហាអយ្យការ) with the following composition:
- Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Clerks and administrative officers, and may include other legal technical officers who work at the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court.

**Article 50.-**
The functions of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court are entrusted to the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court. The Deputy Prosecutor General and the Prosecutor attached to the Appeal Court participate in implementing these functions under the leadership and joint responsibility of the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court.

In case of being busy, sick, or absent, the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court shall assign a Deputy Prosecutor General or, if there is no Deputy Prosecutor General, assign any Prosecutor to replace him.

In case the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court is unable to perform duties or if the position is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall assign any Deputy Prosecutor General to serve as the acting prosecutor general to ensure the functioning of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court until the official appointment of a Prosecutor General.

**Article 51.-**
The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court has power over the Deputy Prosecutor General and Prosecutors attached to the Appeal Court.

The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court assigns and allocates work to the Deputy Prosecutor General and Prosecutors attached to the Appeal Court for various tasks within the General Prosecution Office and the trial panels.

The conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្និដ្ឋាន) of the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Appeal Court must be sent to the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court does not agree with those conclusions and the representative of the general prosecution who made those conclusions still refuses to follow, the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court may assign other Deputy Prosecutor Generals or Prosecutors or assign himself to be the representative of the general prosecution in the hearing.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Appeal Court may make oral remarks freely as he deems in accordance with his conscience. No disciplinary charge can be made against the representative of the prosecution due to his oral remarks during the hearing being different from the written conclusions.

The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court has the right to issue orders (អធិបញ្ជា) to the Prosecutor of the First Instance Court to carry out prosecutions by applying the criminal procedure provisions in force.

## Section 3
## General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court

**Article 52.-**
The General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court is the technical body for the Appeal Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court on:
- Personnel, administrative, and financial work
- Management of court administration for technical assistance to the work programs of the Appeal Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court
- Preparation of budget projects for the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court for review and decision
- Implementing budget work according to the approved projects
- Reporting on personnel, administrative, and financial work regularly to the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court
- Public relations work
- Sending final judgments of the Appeal Court to the Ministry of Justice regularly
- Preparing and sending monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, and annual personnel, administrative, and financial work reports to the Ministry of Justice
- Other tasks assigned by the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court
- Implementing other tasks as defined by legal instruments.

The General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court shall be divided into departments (នាយកដ្ឋាន) led by a director (ប្រធាន) and a number of deputy directors (អនុប្រធាន) as necessary.

The organization and functioning of the General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court shall be defined by Sub-decree upon the request of the Minister of Justice.

**Article 53.-**
The General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court is led by a Secretary General (អគ្គលេខាធិការ) and a number of Deputy Secretaries General (អគ្គលេខាធិការរង) as necessary. The Secretary General shall be appointed by Royal Decree upon the recommendation of the Prime Minister. The Minister of Justice consults with the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court.

The Secretary General shall be selected from civil servants who have experience working in legal, administrative, or financial fields for at least 07 (seven) years.

Deputy Secretaries General and Directors shall be appointed by Sub-decree upon request of the Minister of Justice after consultation with the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court. Deputy Secretaries General and Directors shall be selected from civil servants who have experience working in legal, administrative, or financial fields for at least 05 (five) years.

Officers working at the general secretariat below the Director shall be appointed by a Prokas of the Minister of Justice. Deputy Directors and office chiefs and deputy chiefs shall be selected from civil servants who have experience working in legal, administrative, or financial fields for at least 03 (three) years.

All officers working at the general secretariat, including the Secretary General, shall have their framework at the Ministry of Justice.

**Article 54.-**
The Assembly of each Appeal Court shall take place under the presidency of the President of the Appeal Court and the Prosecutor General of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court to summarize past work results and define directions for the administrative work of the Appeal Court for the following year. The report of the assembly of the Appeal Court shall be sent to the Ministry of Justice.

# Chapter 5
# Incompatibility (វិសមិតភាព)

**Article 74.-**
The function of public prosecution (មុខងារធ្វើបណ្តឹងអាជ្ញា), investigation (មុខងារស៊ើបសួរ), and adjudication (មុខងារជំនុំជម្រះ) shall be separated. Any judge who has handled any case in the capacity of a representative of the prosecution or as an investigating judge cannot adjudicate or participate in the adjudication of that case; otherwise, the judgment or final judgment shall be null and void (មោឃៈ).

**Article 75.-**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating the same case together with one or more judges who are spouses (សហព័ទ្ធ) or blood relatives (ញាតិលោហិត) up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including the 4th degree, or relatives by marriage (ញាតិពន្ធ) up to the 3rd (third) degree, including the 3rd degree, whether in the capacity of a trial judge or a representative of the prosecution. This principle shall also apply to judges in higher courts for cases that were adjudicated by judges in lower courts who are spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th degree, including the 4th degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd degree, including the 3rd degree.

**Article 76.-**
Judges who have spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including the 4th degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including the 3rd degree, serving as a lawyer (មេធាវី) representing any party in a case, cannot participate in the adjudication of that case.

**Article 77.-**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating any case where one party is a spouse or blood relative up to the 6th (sixth) degree, including the 6th degree, or relative by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including the 3rd degree.

**Article 78.-**
Unless otherwise provided by procedural provisions, all parties in a case and individuals with an interest in the case, including the representative of the prosecution, may file a motion for nullity (បណ្តឹងសុំមោឃកម្ម) against a court judgment or final judgment due to a violation of incompatibility rules as specified in Articles 74, 75, 76, and 77 above.

This motion for nullity may be made at any time before the court in appeal procedures by applying the procedural provisions in force. Even if the judgment or final judgment has become final (ស្ថាពរ), the motion for nullity can still be made within 60 (sixty) days from the date of learning about the cause as specified in Articles 74, 75, 76, and 77 above after the judgment or final judgment has become final. In this last case, the motion for nullity must be filed at the court that issued the final judgment or final judgment, but there must be a different composition of trial judges to review and decide on the nullity of that final judgment or final judgment and continue the procedure in accordance with the provisions in force.

# Chapter 6
# Budget for Courts and the Prosecution Office attached to the Courts

**Article 79.-**
The First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court, the Appeal Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court, as well as the Supreme Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court, have separate budget packages for their operations which are within the budget package of the Ministry of Justice.

The President of each Court of all levels is the delegated authorizing officer (អាណាប័កផ្ទេរសិទ្ធិ) from the Minister of Justice.

The management and allocation of the budget for the Courts and the Prosecution Office attached to the Courts as defined in the 1st paragraph above shall be defined by Sub-decree.

# Chapter 7
# Transitional Provisions (អន្តរប្បញ្ញត្តិ)

**Article 80.-**
Courts of all levels and prosecution bodies attached to these Courts established before this Law enters into force shall continue their operations in accordance with the provisions of this Law.

**Article 81.-**
Previous provisions related to Military Courts (តុលាការយោធា) and the Prosecution Office attached to the Military Courts, as well as provisions on jurisdiction and procedure applied for military offense cases, shall continue to be in force after this Law enters into force until there are new provisions to replace them.

**Article 82.-**
Pending the operationalization of other Appeal Courts, the current Appeal Court located in Phnom Penh has territorial jurisdiction extending over the entire territory of the Kingdom of Cambodia.

**Article 83.-**
The operationalization of specialized courts of the First Instance Court as specified in Article 14 of this Law shall be done by a Prokas of the Minister of Justice.

**Article 84.-**
In case the First Instance Court of any province has not been operationalized or cannot be operationalized, the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases in that province shall be entrusted to any First Instance Court by Royal Decree.

**Article 85.-**
When the Commercial Chamber and the Labour Chamber of a higher court cannot operate due to a lack of judges, the adjudication of commercial cases and labour cases is the jurisdiction of the Civil Chamber of that higher court. These tasks shall be managed by the President of the higher court where that specialized chamber belongs.

When any of the above specialized chambers is operationalized in accordance with this Law, other specialized chambers shall transfer cases that are not within their jurisdiction and have not been handled back to the competent specialized chamber.

**Article 86.-**
In case there are not enough judges, investigating judges may continue to perform functions as trial judges, but cannot adjudicate cases they have investigated.

**Article 87.-**
When there is no Administrative Court (សាលាជម្រះក្តីរដ្ឋបាល) yet, the jurisdiction to adjudicate administrative cases shall be entrusted to the Civil Court of the lower court and the Civil Chamber of the higher court.

**Article 88.-**
When there are no commercial advisors or labour advisors yet, the composition for adjudicating commercial cases and labour cases shall follow the provisions on civil procedure.

**Article 89.-**
When there are no commercial procedure provisions yet, the adjudication of commercial cases shall follow the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

When there are no labour procedure provisions yet, the adjudication of labour cases shall follow the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

When there are no administrative procedure provisions yet, the adjudication of administrative cases shall follow the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

**Article 90.-**
During the first 05 (five) years after this Law enters into force, the appointment of leadership of the administrative units of the Courts of all levels can be implemented based on actual qualification conditions.

The Chief Clerk (នាយកក្រឡាបញ្ជី) of the First Instance Court can be appointed as the chief or deputy chief of the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court where he is working.

The Chief Clerk of a higher court can be appointed as the Secretary General or Deputy Secretary General of the General Secretariat of Administration of the higher court where he is working.

**Article 91.-**
Provisions on the judicial system, criminal law, and criminal procedure for implementation in Cambodia during the transitional period dated September 10, 1992, and the Law on the Organization and Activities of the Courts of the State of Cambodia promulgated by Decree No. 06 Kr. dated February 08, 1993, and all provisions contrary to this Law are hereby repealed and replaced by this Law.
