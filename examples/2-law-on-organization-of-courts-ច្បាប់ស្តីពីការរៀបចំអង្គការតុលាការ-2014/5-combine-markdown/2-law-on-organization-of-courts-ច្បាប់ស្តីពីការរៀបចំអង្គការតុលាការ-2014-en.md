## On (ស្តីពី)
### Organization of Courts (ការរៀបចំអង្គការតុលាការ)

# Chapter 1 (ជំពូកទី ១)
# General Provisions (បទប្បញ្ញត្តិទូទៅ)

**Article 1.- (មាត្រា ១.-)**
This Law aims to:
- Guarantee the independence of the judicial power (ធានាឯករាជ្យនៃអំណាចតុលាការ).
- Guarantee the impartiality (អនាគតិ) and protect the rights and freedoms of citizens.
- Guarantee the good functioning of the judicial organizations (អង្គការតុលាការ) and the prosecution organizations (អង្គការអយ្យការ).
- Promote public services to be efficient and fast.
- Guarantee the provision of justice in all types of cases to increase the confidence of citizens and contribute to strengthening social safety.

**Article 2.- (មាត្រា ២.-)**
This Law has the objective to:
- Define the organization and functioning of judicial organizations in all sectors and at all levels which are the adjudicating courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តី) of the Kingdom of Cambodia.
- Define the organization and functioning of the prosecution organizations (អង្គការអយ្យការ) attached to the adjudicating courts at all levels of the Kingdom of Cambodia.
- Define the jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច) of the courts in all sectors and at all levels according to specialization.
- Organize the management work, administrative work, and the functioning process of the courts.

**Article 3.- (មាត្រា ៣.-)**
The adjudicating courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តី) of the Kingdom of Cambodia include:
- First Instance Courts (សាលាដំបូង)
- Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍)
- Supreme Court (តុលាការកំពូល).

The First Instance Court is the lower-level court (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ទាប). The Appeal Court and the Supreme Court are the higher-level courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ខ្ពស់).

Based on actual necessary needs, the establishment of any adjudicating court or other specialized adjudicating courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីឯកទេស) shall be made by law.

**Article 4.- (មាត្រា ៤.-)**
Adjudicating courts in all sectors and at all levels implement the judicial power (អំណាចតុលាការ) covering all cases, including administrative cases (រឿងក្តីរដ្ឋបាល).

**Article 5.- (មាត្រា ៥.-)**
Lower-level adjudicating courts shall be divided into specialized courts (តុលាការជំនាញ). The establishment, abolition, and modification of specialized courts shall be defined by Royal Decree.

The President of the First Instance Court has direct administrative hierarchical power (អំណាចឋានានុក្រមផ្នែករដ្ឋបាល) over all presidents of specialized courts of that First Instance Court. Each president of a specialized court has direct administrative hierarchical power over the judges who are members. The President of the First Instance Court cannot serve as the president of any specialized court. Each president of a specialized court can serve as the president of only one specialized court of that First Instance Court.

Higher-level adjudicating courts shall be divided into specialized chambers (សភាជំនាញ). The establishment, abolition, and modification of specialized chambers shall be defined by Royal Decree.

The President of the higher-level adjudicating court has direct administrative hierarchical power over all presidents of specialized chambers of that higher-level adjudicating court. Each president of a specialized chamber has direct administrative hierarchical power over the judges who are members. The President of the higher-level adjudicating court cannot serve as the president of any specialized chamber. Each president of a specialized chamber can serve as the president of only one specialized chamber of that higher-level adjudicating court.

**Article 6.- (មាត្រា ៦.-)**
Only judges have the right to adjudicate (ជម្រះក្តី). Judges shall fulfill this duty independently (ឯករាជ្យ) while strictly respecting the law and to the best of their heart and conscience (សម្បជញ្ញៈ).

The adjudication of justice shall be done in the name of the Khmer people (ប្រជារាស្ត្រខ្មែរ) according to the procedures and laws in force.

Only the adjudicating courts have the power to judge and issue court decisions (សេចក្តីសម្រេច).

**Article 7.- (មាត្រា ៧.-)**
Hearings (សវនាការ) shall be conducted publicly, except where such publicity could cause danger to public order (សណ្តាប់ធ្នាប់សាធារណៈ) or good customs (ទំនៀមទម្លាប់ល្អ), or there are special provisions in the law stating otherwise.

In all cases, court judgments (សាលក្រម) and verdicts (សាលដីកា) shall be declared in the courtroom (សាលសវនាការ) which is open to the public.

Deliberations (ការពិភាក្សាសម្រេចសេចក្តី) of the chamber shall be kept secret (សម្ងាត់).

Unless there are provisions stating otherwise, the declaration of the court judgment or verdict shall be done by the presiding judge (ប្រធានអង្គជំនុំជម្រះ) or one judge among the judges who are the composition of the chamber.

Every court judgment and verdict must have reasoning (សំអាងហេតុ).

Unless there are provisions stating otherwise, judgments and verdicts shall be signed by the presiding judge and co-signed by the court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) of the hearing.

**Article 8.- (មាត្រា ៨.-)**
Attached to the First Instance Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court (អយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង).

Attached to the Appeal Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court (មហាអយ្យការអមសាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍).

Attached to the Supreme Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court (មហាអយ្យការអមតុលាការកំពូល).

The representative of the prosecution organization must be present at every hearing of criminal cases (រឿងក្តីព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ) and other cases as defined by law.

**Article 9.- (មាត្រា ៩.-)**
In every judicial organization and prosecution organization at all levels, there are court clerks (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) and administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល), and there may be legal specialized officers (មន្ត្រីជំនាញផ្នែកច្បាប់) as assistants as necessary.

Court clerks assist judges and prosecutors at every stage of court and prosecution procedures.

The role and functions of court clerks shall be defined in the Statute of Court Clerks (លក្ខន្តិកៈក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The modalities and conditions for the recruitment of legal specialized officers shall be defined by Sub-decree (អនុក្រឹត្យ).

**Article 10.- (មាត្រា ១០.-)**
At adjudicating courts of all levels, there is an administrative unit which is under the central administration of the Ministry of Justice to support the functioning of the judicial organizations and prosecution organizations.

At the level of the First Instance Court, this administrative unit is called the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court (លេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលសាលាដំបូង), having a rank equal to a Department (នាយកដ្ឋាន).

At the level of the Appeal Court, this administrative unit is called the General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court (អគ្គលេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលសាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍), having a rank equal to a General Department (អគ្គនាយកដ្ឋាន).

At the level of the Supreme Court, this administrative unit is called the General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court (អគ្គលេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលតុលាការកំពូល), having a rank equal to a General Department (អគ្គនាយកដ្ឋាន).

**Article 11.- (មាត្រា ១១.-)**
The Ministry of Justice has the right to oversee the administrative affairs of all adjudicating courts, with a General Department of Court Administration (អគ្គនាយកដ្ឋានកិច្ចការរដ្ឋបាលតុលាការ) as the chief of staff. The Ministry of Justice may issue legal instruments (លិខិតបទដ្ឋានគតិយុត្ត) and various necessary instructions to ensure the better functioning of court administrative management at all levels.

In case of necessity, the Minister of Justice may assign an inspection (អធិការកិច្ច) on any specific issue, and then report to the Supreme Council of Magistracy (ឧត្តមក្រុមប្រឹក្សានៃអង្គចៅក្រម) for review and decision.

Higher-level adjudicating courts have the jurisdiction to oversee the adjudication affairs (កិច្ចការជម្រះក្តី) of lower-level adjudicating courts. This oversight takes place within the framework of remedy procedures (នីតិវិធីឧបាស្រ័យ) in accordance with the provisions on procedures in force.

# Chapter 2 (ជំពូកទី ២)
# First Instance Courts and Prosecution Offices attached to First Instance Courts (សាលាដំបូង និងអយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង)

## Section 1 (ផ្នែកទី ១)
## First Instance Courts (សាលាដំបូង)

### Sub-section 1 (អនុផ្នែកទី ១)
### Organization and Functioning of First Instance Courts (ការរៀបចំ និងការប្រព្រឹត្តទៅរបស់សាលាដំបូង)

**Article 12.- (មាត្រា ១២.-)**
The First Instance Court is the first-level adjudicating court which must be established in Phnom Penh and in every province of the Kingdom of Cambodia. Putting various First Instance Courts into operation shall be done by Royal Decree.

The First Instance Court shall be divided into specialized courts. The First Instance Court is a general adjudicating court (សាលាជម្រះក្តីទូទៅ) which has the jurisdiction to adjudicate all cases, except for cases which are under the jurisdiction of special adjudicating courts or extraordinary adjudicating courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីវិសាមញ្ញ) established by separate laws.

The composition of the First Instance Court includes:
- President (ប្រធាន)
- Vice-president (អនុប្រធាន)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម).

- Vice-president (អនុប្រធាន)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី)
- Administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល) and may have other legal specialized officers who work at the First Instance Court.

**Article 14.- (មាត្រា ១៤.-)**
Each First Instance Court is divided into specialized courts as follows:
- Civil Court (តុលាការរដ្ឋប្បវេណី)
- Criminal Court (តុលាការព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ)
- Commercial Court (តុលាការពាណិជ្ជកម្ម)
- Labour Court (តុលាការការងារ).

Other specialized courts of the First Instance Court may be established based on necessary needs. This establishment shall be done by Royal Decree.

Each specialized court of the First Instance Court issues its decisions autonomously (ស្វ័យភាព) within its jurisdiction in the name of the First Instance Court where that specialized court is located.

**Article 15.- (មាត្រា ១៥.-)**
The composition of each specialized court includes:
- President of the Court (ប្រធានតុលាការ)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The president of each specialized court, including judges and court clerks, shall be appointed by the President of the First Instance Court. The process of these specialized courts shall be defined by the President of the First Instance Court in accordance with the legal instruments in force.

**Article 16.- (មាត្រា ១៦.-)**
Each specialized court of the First Instance Court adjudicates by a single judge (ចៅក្រមទោល) or by a trial chamber (ក្រុមប្រឹក្សាជំនុំជម្រះ) in accordance with the provisions on civil procedure, criminal procedure, or other provisions on procedures in force.

In case any judge has a task (ធុរៈ) or is in a situation of being unable to fulfill duties, that judge may be substituted by a judge in the subsequent order specified in the annual work allocation table of the President of the First Instance Court.

In case any specialized court cannot hold a hearing due to a lack of judges, the President of the First Instance Court shall appoint a judge from another specialized court to fulfill the requirement. If it is necessary to request one or more judges from other First Instance Courts to complete the composition of this specialized court, the President of the First Instance Court shall request the Minister of Justice.

**Article 17.- (មាត្រា ១៧.-)**
The President of the First Instance Court takes necessary management measures for the operation of the First Instance Court. In case the President of the First Instance Court is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of the President of the First Instance Court is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint a Vice-president of the First Instance Court, or if there is no Vice-president, may appoint any judge to serve as the Acting President (ប្រធានស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the First Instance Court until the official appointment of the President of the First Instance Court. The appointment of the President of the First Instance Court shall be made in the shortest period and at most not exceeding 30 (thirty) days.

The President of the First Instance Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Appoint any Vice-president of the First Instance Court to manage the work on their behalf in case of necessity.
- Appoint a Vice-president of the First Instance Court to be in charge as the president of each specialized court based on skills or work experience. In case of a lack of Vice-presidents, the President of the First Instance Court may appoint any judge who has a high rank (ឋានន្តរស័ក្តិក) or extensive experience to be the president of a specialized court.
- Define the order for substituting judges in each specialized court and in the trial chambers of these specialized courts in case any judge is absent or has a task.
- Appoint and change court clerks in each specialized court according to actual necessary needs.

**Article 18.- (មាត្រា ១៨.-)**
In case of necessity, the Minister of Justice may decide to allow the First Instance Court to hold hearings outside its office location upon request from the President of the First Instance Court.

### Sub-section 2 (អនុផ្នែកទី ២)
### Jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច)

**Article 19.- (មាត្រា ១៩.-)**
The First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases where the law grants jurisdiction to other adjudicating courts.

In case of necessity, to guarantee public interest, safety, social security, and the good process of court justice, the Minister of Justice may decide to transfer the jurisdiction for handling cases from one First Instance Court to another.

In case of jurisdictional disputes (វិវាទសមត្ថកិច្ច) between various specialized courts within the First Instance Court, the President of the First Instance Court shall convene a meeting of the relevant presidents of specialized courts by their discretion or upon request from any president of a specialized court to decide on the jurisdiction. The President of the First Instance Court decides on this jurisdictional dispute by a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច). This decision is a final decision (សេចក្តីសម្រេចបិទផ្លូវតវ៉ា).

**Article 20.- (មាត្រា ២០.-)**
The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates all criminal cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure, unless there are legal provisions stating otherwise.

The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates criminal cases by a single judge or by a trial chamber in accordance with the provisions on criminal procedure.

The Investigation Chamber (មន្ទីរស៊ើបសួរ) of the First Instance Court is a part of the Criminal Court and has the jurisdiction to investigate criminal cases by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure. The President of the First Instance Court shall appoint at least 02 (two) judges by a decision to fulfill the role of investigating judges (ចៅក្រមស៊ើបសួរ) at the Investigation Chamber of the Criminal Court of the First Instance Court. This decision shall also define the numerical order for receiving work. In case there is no investigating judge or a lack of investigating judges, the President of the First Instance Court may appoint judges of other specialized courts to fulfill investigation functions.

In case of necessity, investigating judges may perform duties as judges at other specialized courts as appointed by the President of the First Instance Court.

**Article 21.- (មាត្រា ២១.-)**
The Civil Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate civil cases and all cases for which there is no provision granting jurisdiction to other specialized courts by implementing the provisions on civil procedure.

The Civil Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates civil cases by a single judge or by a trial chamber by implementing the provisions on civil procedure.

**Article 22.- (មាត្រា ២២.-)**
The Commercial Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate commercial cases, including bankruptcy cases (រឿងក្តីក្ស័យធន) in commercial matters by implementing the provisions on commercial procedure.

**Article 23.- (មាត្រា ២៣.-)**
The Commercial Court adjudicates with 03 (three) judges accompanied by 02 (two) advisors (ទីប្រឹក្សា) who are merchants or persons with knowledge of commercial law in the case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value from 1,000,000,000 (one billion) Riels upward.

The Commercial Court adjudicates with 01 (one) judge accompanied by 02 (two) advisors who are merchants or persons with knowledge of commercial law in the case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value from 100,000,000 (one hundred million) Riels to below 1,000,000,000 (one billion) Riels.

The Commercial Court adjudicates with 01 (one) judge without the need for participation from commercial advisors in the case of commercial cases where the object of the lawsuit has a value below 100,000,000 (one hundred million) Riels.

The judgment (សាលក្រម) of the Commercial Court shall be decided by 01 (one) judge or 03 (three) judges according to paragraphs 1 and 2 above after consulting with the commercial advisors.

Commercial advisors do not fulfill their duties permanently (អចិន្ត្រៃយ៍) in the Commercial Court of the First Instance Court. Commercial advisors shall come to fulfill their duties at the First Instance Court upon invitation from the President of the Commercial Court of the First Instance Court.

**Article 24.- (មាត្រា ២៤.-)**
The modalities of appointment and the fulfillment of duties of commercial advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree (អនុក្រឹត្យ) upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the Minister of Commerce.

**Article 25.- (មាត្រា ២៥.-)**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate labour cases by implementing the provisions on labour procedure.

**Article 26.- (មាត្រា ២៦.-)**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall adjudicate by 01 (one) judge accompanied by 02 (two) labour advisors (ទីប្រឹក្សាការងារ), one of whom is an employee (កម្មករនិយោជិត) and the other is an employer (និយោជក).

The judgment (សាលក្រម) of the Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall be decided by 01 (one) judge after consulting with the labour advisors.

Labour advisors do not fulfill their duties permanently in the Labour Court of the First Instance Court. Labour advisors shall participate in fulfilling their duties at the First Instance Court upon invitation from the President of the Labour Court.

**Article 27.- (មាត្រា ២៧.-)**
The competent Labour Court is the Labour Court of the First Instance Court at the place of the labour dispute (វិវាទការងារ).

Despite the definition of jurisdiction as in the above paragraph, the employee may file a complaint to the First Instance Court as defined below, unless there are provisions stating otherwise:
- At their residence (លំនៅឋាន) or
- At the location of the head office or primary place of business of the company or
- At the residence of the representative or person in charge of the company if there is no head office or primary place of business of the company.

**Article 28.- (មាត្រា ២៨.-)**
The modalities of election and the fulfillment of duties of labour advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the Minister in charge of the labour sector.

## Section 2 (ផ្នែកទី ២)
## Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court (អយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង)

សមាសភាពអយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង រួមមាន៖
- Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី)
- Administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល) and may have other legal specialized officers who work at the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

**Article 30.- (មាត្រា ៣០.-)**
The prosecution organization is an indivisible body (អង្គអវិភាគ). The prosecution has the function to conduct public action (បណ្តឹងអាជ្ញា) and request the implementation of the law before the investigation jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការស៊ើបសួរ) and adjudication jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការជំនុំជម្រះ), as well as implementing other functions as defined by laws and regulations in force.

The Prosecutor and Deputy Prosecutor are the representatives of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court where they work. Deputy Prosecutors participate in implementing the functions of the prosecution under the leadership and collective responsibility (ទទួលខុសត្រូវរួម) of the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

In case of a task, illness, or absence, the Prosecutor shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor to substitute. In case the Prosecutor is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of Prosecutor is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor to serve as the Acting Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញាស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court until the official appointment of the Prosecutor.

**Article 31.- (មាត្រា ៣១.-)**
The Prosecutor has power over all Deputy Prosecutors within their jurisdiction. The Prosecutor appoints and allocates work to all Deputy Prosecutors.

The Prosecutor has the right to issue orders (អធិបញ្ញា) to Deputy Prosecutors to prosecute or to assign to prosecute or to make conclusions as they deem appropriate. The conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្នីដ្ឋាន) of the Deputy Prosecutor must be sent to the Prosecutor for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor does not agree with that conclusion, and the representative of the prosecution who makes that conclusion still refuses to comply, the Prosecutor may appoint another Deputy Prosecutor or appoint themselves to be the representative of the prosecution in the hearing of the First Instance Court.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor may freely make oral observations (ការកត់សម្គាល់ដោយផ្ទាល់មាត់) according to what they deem to be in accordance with their conscience (សតិសម្បជញ្ញៈ). No disciplinary prosecution can be made against the representative of the prosecution because of their oral observations in the hearing being different from the written conclusions.

## ផ្នែកទី ៣
## Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court (លេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលសាលាដំបូង)

**Article 32.- (មាត្រា ៣២.-)**
The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court is the chief of staff to the First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court on:
- Personnel, administrative, and financial work.
- Court administrative management work for technical assistance to the work programs of the First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.
- Budget project preparation work for the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor to review and decide.
- Regularly reporting on personnel, administrative, and financial work to the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor.
- Implementing budget work according to the approved projects.
- Public relations work.
- Periodic submission of judgments (សាលក្រម) of the First Instance Court to the Ministry of Justice.
- Preparing and submitting monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, and annual personnel, administrative, and financial work reports to the Ministry of Justice.
- Other work assigned by the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.
- Implementing other tasks as defined by legal instruments.

The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court shall be divided into offices (ការិយាល័យ) according to actual necessary numbers. An office is led by one head (ប្រធាន) and several deputy heads (អនុប្រធាន) as necessary.

The organization and functioning of the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court shall be defined by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice.

**Article 33.- (មាត្រា ៣៣.-)**
The Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court is led by one head and several deputy heads as necessary. The Head of the Secretariat shall be appointed by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

The officials working at the Secretariat below the rank of the Head of the Secretariat shall be appointed by Prokas (ប្រកាស) of the Minister of Justice.

The Head and Deputy Head of the Secretariat shall be selected from civil servants who have at least 05 (five) years of work experience in legal, administrative, or financial specializations. The Head and Deputy Head of offices shall be selected from civil servants who have at least 03 (three) years of work experience in legal, administrative, or financial specializations.

All officials working at the Secretariat, including the Head of the Secretariat, must have their framework (ក្របខណ្ឌ) at the Ministry of Justice.

**Article 34.- (មាត្រា ៣៤.-)**
The general assembly (សន្និបាត) of the First Instance Court takes place under the presidency of the President of the First Instance Court and the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court to summarize past work results and define the administrative work direction of the First Instance Court for the following year. The general assembly report of the First Instance Court shall be sent to the Ministry of Justice.

# Chapter 3 (ជំពូកទី ៣)
# Appeal Courts and General Prosecution Offices attached to Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍ និងមហាអយ្យការអមសាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍)

## Section 1 (ផ្នែកទី ១)
## Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍)

### Sub-section 1 (អនុផ្នែកទី ១)
### Organization and Functioning of Appeal Courts (ការរៀបចំ និងការប្រព្រឹត្តទៅរបស់សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍)

**Article 35.- (មាត្រា ៣៥.-)**
The Appeal Court is the second-level adjudicating court.

Appeal Courts include the Phnom Penh Appeal Court and Regional Appeal Courts (សាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍តំបន់). Putting each Regional Appeal Court into operation and defining the territorial jurisdiction of these Regional Appeal Courts shall be done by Royal Decree.

**Article 36.- (មាត្រា ៣៦.-)**
The composition of an Appeal Court includes:
- President (ប្រធាន)
- Vice-president (អនុប្រធាន)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) and administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល), and may have other legal specialized officers who work at the Appeal Court.

**Article 37.- (មាត្រា ៣៧.-)**
An Appeal Court has specialized chambers (សភាជំនាញ) as follows:
- Criminal Chamber (សភាព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ)
- Civil Chamber (សភារដ្ឋប្បវេណី)
- Investigation Chamber (សភាស៊ើបសួរ)
- Commercial Chamber (សភាពាណិជ្ជកម្ម)
- Labour Chamber (សភាការងារ).

In case of necessity, other specialized chambers of the Appeal Court may be established by Royal Decree.

Each chamber issues its decisions autonomously within its jurisdiction in the name of the Appeal Court where the chamber is located.

**Article 38.- (មាត្រា ៣៨.-)**
The composition of each chamber includes:
- President of the Chamber (ប្រធានសភា)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម) and
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The president of each chamber shall be appointed by the President of the Appeal Court. The number of judges, court clerks, and the process of these chambers shall be defined by the President of the Appeal Court.

**Article 39.- (មាត្រា ៣៩.-)**
Each chamber of the Appeal Court adjudicates with 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, in accordance with the provisions on procedures in force.

In any case which the Supreme Court has remanded (បង្វិលមកវិញ), the Appeal Court shall adjudicate in a hearing which consists of 05 (five) judges, all of whom are judges who did not participate in adjudicating that case before, by implementing the provisions on procedures in force.

**Article 40.- (មាត្រា ៤០.-)**
The President of the Appeal Court takes necessary management measures for the operation of the Appeal Court.

The President of the Appeal Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Appoint any Vice-president of the Appeal Court to manage the work on their behalf in case of necessity.
- Appoint a Vice-president of the Appeal Court to be in charge as the president of each chamber according to skills and work experience. In case of a lack of Vice-presidents of the Appeal Court, the President of the Appeal Court may appoint any judge who has a high rank or extensive work experience to be the president of a chamber.
- Define the order for substituting judges in the trial chambers of each chamber in case any judge is absent or has a task.
- Appoint and change court clerks in each chamber according to actual necessity.

### Sub-section 2 (អនុផ្នែកទី ២)
### Jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច)

**Article 41.- (មាត្រា ៤១.-)**
The Appeal Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases where the law grants jurisdiction to other courts.

**Article 42.- (មាត្រា ៤២.-)**

# Chapter 5 (ជំពូកទី ៥)
# Incompatibility (វិសមិតភាព)

**Article 74.- (មាត្រា ៧៤.-)**
The functions of prosecution, investigation, and adjudication must be separated from each other. Any judge who has already been in charge of handling any case in the capacity of a representative of the prosecution or as an investigating judge cannot adjudicate or participate in adjudicating that case which they handled; otherwise, the judgment or verdict shall be null and void.

**Article 75.- (មាត្រា ៧៥.-)**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating the same case together with one or more judges who are spouses (សហព័ទ្ធ) or blood relatives (ញាតិលោហិត) up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage (ញាតិពន្ធ) up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree, whether in the capacity of an adjudicating judge or a representative of the prosecution. This principle shall also be applied to judges in higher courts for cases adjudicated by judges in lower courts who are spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree.

**Article 76.- (មាត្រា ៧៦.-)**
All judges who have spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree, serving as a lawyer for any party in any case cannot participate in adjudicating that case.

**Article 77.- (មាត្រា ៧៧.-)**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating any case where one party is a spouse or blood relative up to the 6th (sixth) degree, including this 6th (sixth) degree, or relative by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree.

**Article 78.- (មាត្រា ៧៨.-)**
Unless there are provisions on procedures stating otherwise, all parties in a case and persons who have an interest in a case, including the representative of the prosecution, can file a motion for nullity (ប្តឹងសុំមោឃកម្ម) against a judgment or verdict of a court due to a violation of the rules on incompatibility as stated in Article 74, Article 75, Article 76, and Article 77 above.

This motion for nullity can be made at any time before the court in the recourse procedure (នីតិវិធីឧបាស្រ័យ) by implementing the provisions on procedures in force. Even if that judgment or verdict has become final (ស្ថាពរ), the motion for nullity can still be made within 60 (sixty) days from the date of knowing about the grounds stated in Article 74, Article 75, Article 76, and Article 77 above after the judgment or verdict has become final. In the latter case, the motion for nullity must be filed at the court that issued the final judgment or verdict, but there must be a different composition of adjudicating judges to review and decide on the nullity of that final judgment or verdict and to implement further procedures in accordance with the provisions in force.

# Chapter 6 (ជំពូកទី ៦)
# Budgets of Courts and Prosecution Offices attached to Courts (ថវិកាសាលាជម្រះក្តី និងអយ្យការអមសាលាជម្រះក្តី)

**Article 79.- (មាត្រា ៧៩.-)**
The First Instance Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court, the Appeal Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court, as well as the Supreme Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court, have separate budget packages for operation, which are located within the budget package of the Ministry of Justice.

The President of the court at all levels is an orderer (អាណាប័ក) delegated by the Minister of Justice.

The management and allocation of the budgets of courts and prosecution offices attached to courts as defined in the 1st paragraph above shall be defined by Sub-decree.

# Chapter 7 (ជំពូកទី ៧)
# Inter-provisions (អន្តរប្បញ្ញត្តិ)

**Article 80.- (មាត្រា ៨០.-)**
Courts at all levels as well as prosecution organizations attached to these courts which were established before this law enters into force shall continue their operation in accordance with the provisions of this law.

**Article 81.- (មាត្រា ៨១.-)**
Previous provisions related to the Military Court and the Prosecution Office attached to the Military Court, as well as provisions on jurisdiction and procedure applied for the processing of military offense cases, shall continue to have the force of implementation after this law enters into force until there are new provisions to replace them.

**Article 82.- (មាត្រា ៨២.-)**
While waiting for the putting into operation of other Appeal Courts, the current Appeal Court located in Phnom Penh has territorial jurisdiction extending over the entire territory of the Kingdom of Cambodia.

**Article 83.- (មាត្រា ៨៣.-)**
The putting into operation of various specialized courts of the First Instance Court as stated in Article 14 of this law shall be done by Prokas of the Minister of Justice.

**Article 84.- (មាត្រា ៨៤.-)**
In case the First Instance Court of any province has not yet been put into operation or cannot be put into operation, the jurisdiction to adjudicate all cases in that province shall be granted to any First Instance Court by Royal Decree.

**Article 85.- (មាត្រា ៨៥.-)**
When the Commercial Chamber and the Labour Chamber of the higher courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ខ្ពស់) cannot operate due to a lack of judges, the adjudication of commercial cases and labour cases is the jurisdiction of the Civil Chamber of the higher courts. These tasks shall be managed by the President of the higher court where the specialized chamber is located.

When any specialized chamber above operates in accordance with this law, other specialized chambers must transfer cases that are not within their jurisdiction and have not yet been handled back to the specialized chamber that has jurisdiction.

**Article 86.- (មាត្រា ៨៦.-)**
In case there are not yet enough judges, an investigating judge can continue to fulfill the function as an adjudicating judge, but cannot adjudicate a case which they investigated.

**Article 87.- (មាត្រា ៨៧.-)**
In case there is not yet an administrative court, the jurisdiction to adjudicate administrative cases shall be granted to the civil courts of the lower courts (សាលាជម្រះក្តីជាន់ទាប) and the Civil Chamber of the higher courts.

**Article 88.- (មាត្រា ៨៨.-)**
When there are not yet commercial advisors or labour advisors, the composition for adjudicating commercial cases and labour cases shall implement the provisions on civil procedure.

**Article 89.- (មាត្រា ៨៩.-)**
When there are not yet provisions on commercial procedure, the adjudication of commercial cases shall implement the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

When there are not yet provisions on labour procedure, the adjudication of labour cases shall implement the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

When there are not yet provisions on administrative procedure, the adjudication of administrative cases shall implement the provisions on civil procedure and other legal provisions in force.

**Article 90.- (មាត្រា ៩០.-)**
In the first 05 (five) years after this law enters into force, the appointment of heads of administrative units of courts at all levels can be implemented according to actual qualification conditions.

 The Director of Clerks (នាយកក្រឡាបញ្ជី) of the First Instance Court can be appointed as the head or deputy head of the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court where they are currently working.

The Director of Clerks of the higher courts can be appointed as the Secretary General or Deputy Secretary General of the General Secretariat of Administration of the higher courts where they are currently working.

# Chapter 8 (ជំពូកទី ៨)
# Final Provisions (អវសានប្បញ្ញត្តិ)

**Article 91.- (មាត្រា ៩១.-)**
Provisions on the judicial system, criminal law, and criminal procedure for implementation in Cambodia during the transitional period dated September 10, 1992, and the Law on the Organization and Activities of the Courts of the State of Cambodia promulgated by Decree No. 06 Kr dated February 08, 1993, as well as any provisions contrary to this law, shall be nullified and replaced by this law.
