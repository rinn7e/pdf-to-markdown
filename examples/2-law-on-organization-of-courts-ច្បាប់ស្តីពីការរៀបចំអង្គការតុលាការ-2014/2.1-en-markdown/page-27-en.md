**Article 63.- (មាត្រា ៦៣.-)**
A joint chamber (សភារួមក្រុម) may be established to operate by a decision of the President of the Supreme Court on their own initiative or upon request from any president of a chamber when any case raises an issue that falls under the jurisdiction of many chambers or if that issue has received conflicting solutions before different chambers of the Supreme Court. The joint chamber operates upon invitation of the President of the Supreme Court.

The decision of the joint chamber regarding jurisdiction between various chambers and legal disputes has binding power for all chambers of the Supreme Court and lower-level courts to follow.

When a joint chamber is established, this chamber must have a composition of at least 09 (nine) judges who are equally divided among the judges representing the relevant chambers. The joint chamber is under the presidency of the President of the Supreme Court.

The President of the Supreme Court issues a decision in accordance with the decision of the joint chamber. The decision on jurisdictional disputes and legal disputes is a final decision (សេចក្តីសម្រេចបិទផ្លូវតវ៉ា).

**Article 64.- (មាត្រា ៦៤.-)**
The Criminal Chamber of the Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals (បណ្តឹងសាទុក្ខ) against criminal verdicts of the Appeal Court by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure in force.

**Article 65.- (មាត្រា ៦៥.-)**
The Civil Chamber of the Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against verdicts and objections against decisions in civil matters of the Appeal Court, as well as on appeals against civil judgments of the First Instance Court and other cases by implementing the provisions on civil procedure in force.

**Article 66.- (មាត្រា ៦៦.-)**
The Commercial Chamber of the Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against verdicts and objections against decisions in commercial matters of the Appeal Court by implementing the provisions on commercial procedure.

**Article 67.- (មាត្រា ៦៧.-)**
The Labour Chamber of the Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against verdicts and objections against decisions in labour matters of the Appeal Court by implementing the provisions on labour procedure.
