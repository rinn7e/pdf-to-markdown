**Article 25.- (មាត្រា ២៥.-)**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate labour cases by implementing the provisions on labour procedure.

**Article 26.- (មាត្រា ២៦.-)**
The Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall adjudicate by 01 (one) judge accompanied by 02 (two) labour advisors (ទីប្រឹក្សាការងារ), one of whom is an employee (កម្មករនិយោជិត) and the other is an employer (និយោជក).

The judgment (សាលក្រម) of the Labour Court of the First Instance Court shall be decided by 01 (one) judge after consulting with the labour advisors.

Labour advisors do not fulfill their duties permanently in the Labour Court of the First Instance Court. Labour advisors shall participate in fulfilling their duties at the First Instance Court upon invitation from the President of the Labour Court.

**Article 27.- (មាត្រា ២៧.-)**
The competent Labour Court is the Labour Court of the First Instance Court at the place of the labour dispute (វិវាទការងារ).

Despite the definition of jurisdiction as in the above paragraph, the employee may file a complaint to the First Instance Court as defined below, unless there are provisions stating otherwise:
- At their residence (លំនៅឋាន) or
- At the location of the head office or primary place of business of the company or
- At the residence of the representative or person in charge of the company if there is no head office or primary place of business of the company.

**Article 28.- (មាត្រា ២៨.-)**
The modalities of election and the fulfillment of duties of labour advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the Minister in charge of the labour sector.
