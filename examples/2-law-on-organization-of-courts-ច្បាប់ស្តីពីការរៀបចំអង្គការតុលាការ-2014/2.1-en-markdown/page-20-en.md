**Article 45.- (មាត្រា ៤៥.-)**
The Investigation Chamber has the jurisdiction to resolve appeals against investigation acts or decisions (ដីកាសម្រេច) of investigating judges and other cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure in force. The decision of the Investigation Chamber shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president.

**Article 46.- (មាត្រា ៤៦.-)**
The Commercial Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and objections against decisions of the First Instance Court in commercial cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on commercial procedure. The Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court shall adjudicate by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, accompanied by 02 (two) advisors who are merchants or persons with knowledge of commercial law and who were not commercial advisors in the same case adjudicated by the First Instance Court and the Appeal Court. The verdict (សាលដីកា) of the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court shall be made by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, after consulting with the commercial advisors.

Commercial advisors do not fulfill their duties permanently in the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court. Commercial advisors shall come to fulfill their duties upon invitation from the President of the Commercial Chamber of the Appeal Court only.

The modalities of appointment and the fulfillment of duties of commercial advisors shall be defined by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the Minister of Commerce.

**Article 47.- (មាត្រា ៤៧.-)**
The Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and objections against decisions of the First Instance Court in labour cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on labour procedure. The Labour Chamber of the Appeal Court shall adjudicate by 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, accompanied by 02 (two) advisors, one of whom is an employee and the other is an employer, and who were not labour advisors in the same case adjudicated by the First Instance Court and the Appeal Court.
