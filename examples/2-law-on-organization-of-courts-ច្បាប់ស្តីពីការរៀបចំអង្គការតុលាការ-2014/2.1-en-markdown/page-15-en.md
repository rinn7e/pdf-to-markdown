សមាសភាពអយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង រួមមាន៖
- Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី)
- Administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល) and may have other legal specialized officers who work at the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

**Article 30.- (មាត្រា ៣០.-)**
The prosecution organization is an indivisible body (អង្គអវិភាគ). The prosecution has the function to conduct public action (បណ្តឹងអាជ្ញា) and request the implementation of the law before the investigation jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការស៊ើបសួរ) and adjudication jurisdiction (យុត្តាធិការជំនុំជម្រះ), as well as implementing other functions as defined by laws and regulations in force.

The Prosecutor and Deputy Prosecutor are the representatives of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court where they work. Deputy Prosecutors participate in implementing the functions of the prosecution under the leadership and collective responsibility (ទទួលខុសត្រូវរួម) of the Prosecutor of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court.

In case of a task, illness, or absence, the Prosecutor shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor to substitute. In case the Prosecutor is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of Prosecutor is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor to serve as the Acting Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញាស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court until the official appointment of the Prosecutor.

**Article 31.- (មាត្រា ៣១.-)**
The Prosecutor has power over all Deputy Prosecutors within their jurisdiction. The Prosecutor appoints and allocates work to all Deputy Prosecutors.

The Prosecutor has the right to issue orders (អធិបញ្ញា) to Deputy Prosecutors to prosecute or to assign to prosecute or to make conclusions as they deem appropriate. The conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្នីដ្ឋាន) of the Deputy Prosecutor must be sent to the Prosecutor for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor does not agree with that conclusion, and the representative of the prosecution who makes that conclusion still refuses to comply, the Prosecutor may appoint another Deputy Prosecutor or appoint themselves to be the representative of the prosecution in the hearing of the First Instance Court.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor may freely make oral observations (ការកត់សម្គាល់ដោយផ្ទាល់មាត់) according to what they deem to be in accordance with their conscience (សតិសម្បជញ្ញៈ). No disciplinary prosecution can be made against the representative of the prosecution because of their oral observations in the hearing being different from the written conclusions.
