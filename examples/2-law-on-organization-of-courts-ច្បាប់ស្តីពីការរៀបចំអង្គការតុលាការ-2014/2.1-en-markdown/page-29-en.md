The conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្នីដ្ឋាន) of the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Supreme Court must be sent to the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court does not agree with that conclusion, and the representative of the General Prosecution Office who makes that conclusion still refuses to comply, the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court may appoint another Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor or appoint themselves to be the representative of the General Prosecution Office in the hearing.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Supreme Court may freely make oral observations according to what they deem to be in accordance with their conscience. No disciplinary prosecution can be made against the representative of the General Prosecution Office because of their oral observations during the hearing being different from the written conclusions.

## Section 3 (ផ្នែកទី ៣)
## General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court (អគ្គលេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលតុលាការកំពូល)

**Article 71.- (មាត្រា ៧១.-)**
The General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court is the chief of staff to the Supreme Court and the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court on:
- Personnel, administrative, and financial work.
- Court administrative management work for technical assistance to the work programs of the Court.
- Budget project preparation work for the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court to review and decide.
- Regularly reporting on personnel, administrative, and financial work to the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court.
- Public relations work.
- Periodic submission of verdicts (សាលដីកា) of the Supreme Court to the Ministry of Justice.
- Preparing and submitting monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, and annual personnel, administrative, and financial work reports to the Ministry of Justice.
- Implementing budget work according to the approved projects.
- Other work assigned by the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court.
- Implementing other tasks as defined by legal instruments.
