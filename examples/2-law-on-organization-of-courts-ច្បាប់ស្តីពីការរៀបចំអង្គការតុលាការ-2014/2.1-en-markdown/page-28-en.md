## Section 2 (ផ្នែកទី ២)
## General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court (មហាអយ្យការអមតុលាការកំពូល)

**Article 68.- (មាត្រា ៦៨.-)**
The composition of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court includes:
- Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Deputy Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញារង)
- Prosecutor (ព្រះរាជអាជ្ញា)
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) and administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល), and may have other legal specialized officers who work at the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court.

**Article 69.- (មាត្រា ៦៩.-)**
The functions of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court are entrusted to the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court. Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Supreme Court participate in implementing these functions under the leadership and collective responsibility of the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court.

In case of a task, illness, or absence, the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court shall appoint one Deputy Prosecutor General, or if there is no Deputy Prosecutor General, may appoint any Prosecutor to substitute.

In case the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of the Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor General to serve as the Acting Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញាស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court until the official appointment of the Prosecutor General.

**Article 70.- (មាត្រា ៧០.-)**
The Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court has management power over Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Supreme Court. The Prosecutor General attached to the Supreme Court appoints and allocates work to Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Supreme Court in various tasks of the General Prosecution Office and in the trial chambers.
