**Article 40.- (មាត្រា ៤០.-)**
The President of the Appeal Court takes necessary management measures for the operation of the Appeal Court.

The President of the Appeal Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Appoint any Vice-president of the Appeal Court to manage the work on their behalf in case of necessity.
- Appoint a Vice-president of the Appeal Court to be in charge as the president of each chamber according to skills and work experience. In case of a lack of Vice-presidents of the Appeal Court, the President of the Appeal Court may appoint any judge who has a high rank or extensive work experience to be the president of a chamber.
- Define the order for substituting judges in the trial chambers of each chamber in case any judge is absent or has a task.
- Appoint and change court clerks in each chamber according to actual necessity.

### Sub-section 2 (អនុផ្នែកទី ២)
### Jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច)

**Article 41.- (មាត្រា ៤១.-)**
The Appeal Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases where the law grants jurisdiction to other courts.

**Article 42.- (មាត្រា ៤២.-)**
The Appeal Court decides on both facts (អង្គហេតុ) and law (អង្គច្បាប់) on cases within its jurisdiction in accordance with the laws and regulations in force.

**Article 43.- (មាត្រា ៤៣.-)**
The Criminal Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against criminal judgments (សាលក្រមព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ) of the First Instance Court and other cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure in force.

**Article 44.- (មាត្រា ៤៤.-)**
The Civil Chamber has the jurisdiction to adjudicate on appeals against judgments and objections (បណ្តឹងជំទាស់) against decisions of the First Instance Court in civil cases and other cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on civil procedure in force.
