In all cases, court judgments (សាលក្រម) and verdicts (សាលដីកា) shall be declared in the courtroom (សាលសវនាការ) which is open to the public.

Deliberations (ការពិភាក្សាសម្រេចសេចក្តី) of the chamber shall be kept secret (សម្ងាត់).

Unless there are provisions stating otherwise, the declaration of the court judgment or verdict shall be done by the presiding judge (ប្រធានអង្គជំនុំជម្រះ) or one judge among the judges who are the composition of the chamber.

Every court judgment and verdict must have reasoning (សំអាងហេតុ).

Unless there are provisions stating otherwise, judgments and verdicts shall be signed by the presiding judge and co-signed by the court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) of the hearing.

**Article 8.- (មាត្រា ៨.-)**
Attached to the First Instance Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the Prosecution Office attached to the First Instance Court (អយ្យការអមសាលាដំបូង).

Attached to the Appeal Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court (មហាអយ្យការអមសាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍).

Attached to the Supreme Court, there shall be a prosecution organization called the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court (មហាអយ្យការអមតុលាការកំពូល).

The representative of the prosecution organization must be present at every hearing of criminal cases (រឿងក្តីព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ) and other cases as defined by law.

**Article 9.- (មាត្រា ៩.-)**
In every judicial organization and prosecution organization at all levels, there are court clerks (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី) and administrative officers (មន្ត្រីរដ្ឋបាល), and there may be legal specialized officers (មន្ត្រីជំនាញផ្នែកច្បាប់) as assistants as necessary.

Court clerks assist judges and prosecutors at every stage of court and prosecution procedures.

The role and functions of court clerks shall be defined in the Statute of Court Clerks (លក្ខន្តិកៈក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The modalities and conditions for the recruitment of legal specialized officers shall be defined by Sub-decree (អនុក្រឹត្យ).

**Article 10.- (មាត្រា ១០.-)**
At adjudicating courts of all levels, there is an administrative unit which is under the central administration of the Ministry of Justice to support the functioning of the judicial organizations and prosecution organizations.

At the level of the First Instance Court, this administrative unit is called the Administrative Secretariat of the First Instance Court (លេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលសាលាដំបូង), having a rank equal to a Department (នាយកដ្ឋាន).

At the level of the Appeal Court, this administrative unit is called the General Secretariat of Administration of the Appeal Court (អគ្គលេខាធិការដ្ឋានរដ្ឋបាលសាលាឧទ្ធរណ៍), having a rank equal to a General Department (អគ្គនាយកដ្ឋាន).
