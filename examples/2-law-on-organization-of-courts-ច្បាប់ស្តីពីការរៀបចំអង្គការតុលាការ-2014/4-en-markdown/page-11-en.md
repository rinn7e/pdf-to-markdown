In case any specialized court cannot hold a hearing due to a lack of judges, the President of the First Instance Court shall appoint a judge from another specialized court to fulfill the requirement. If it is necessary to request one or more judges from other First Instance Courts to complete the composition of this specialized court, the President of the First Instance Court shall request the Minister of Justice.

**Article 17.- (មាត្រា ១៧.-)**
The President of the First Instance Court takes necessary management measures for the operation of the First Instance Court. In case the President of the First Instance Court is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of the President of the First Instance Court is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint a Vice-president of the First Instance Court, or if there is no Vice-president, may appoint any judge to serve as the Acting President (ប្រធានស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the First Instance Court until the official appointment of the President of the First Instance Court. The appointment of the President of the First Instance Court shall be made in the shortest period and at most not exceeding 30 (thirty) days.

The President of the First Instance Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Appoint any Vice-president of the First Instance Court to manage the work on their behalf in case of necessity.
- Appoint a Vice-president of the First Instance Court to be in charge as the president of each specialized court based on skills or work experience. In case of a lack of Vice-presidents, the President of the First Instance Court may appoint any judge who has a high rank (ឋានន្តរស័ក្តិក) or extensive experience to be the president of a specialized court.
- Define the order for substituting judges in each specialized court and in the trial chambers of these specialized courts in case any judge is absent or has a task.
- Appoint and change court clerks in each specialized court according to actual necessary needs.

**Article 18.- (មាត្រា ១៨.-)**
In case of necessity, the Minister of Justice may decide to allow the First Instance Court to hold hearings outside its office location upon request from the President of the First Instance Court.
