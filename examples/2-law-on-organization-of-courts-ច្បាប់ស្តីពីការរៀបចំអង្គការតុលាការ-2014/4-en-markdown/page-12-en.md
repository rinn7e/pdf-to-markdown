### Sub-section 2 (អនុផ្នែកទី ២)
### Jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច)

**Article 19.- (មាត្រា ១៩.-)**
The First Instance Court has the jurisdiction to adjudicate cases within its territorial jurisdiction, except for cases where the law grants jurisdiction to other adjudicating courts.

In case of necessity, to guarantee public interest, safety, social security, and the good process of court justice, the Minister of Justice may decide to transfer the jurisdiction for handling cases from one First Instance Court to another.

In case of jurisdictional disputes (វិវាទសមត្ថកិច្ច) between various specialized courts within the First Instance Court, the President of the First Instance Court shall convene a meeting of the relevant presidents of specialized courts by their discretion or upon request from any president of a specialized court to decide on the jurisdiction. The President of the First Instance Court decides on this jurisdictional dispute by a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច). This decision is a final decision (សេចក្តីសម្រេចបិទផ្លូវតវ៉ា).

**Article 20.- (មាត្រា ២០.-)**
The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates all criminal cases within its jurisdiction by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure, unless there are legal provisions stating otherwise.

The Criminal Court of the First Instance Court adjudicates criminal cases by a single judge or by a trial chamber in accordance with the provisions on criminal procedure.

The Investigation Chamber (មន្ទីរស៊ើបសួរ) of the First Instance Court is a part of the Criminal Court and has the jurisdiction to investigate criminal cases by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure. The President of the First Instance Court shall appoint at least 02 (two) judges by a decision to fulfill the role of investigating judges (ចៅក្រមស៊ើបសួរ) at the Investigation Chamber of the Criminal Court of the First Instance Court. This decision shall also define the numerical order for receiving work. In case there is no investigating judge or a lack of investigating judges, the President of the First Instance Court may appoint judges of other specialized courts to fulfill investigation functions.

In case of necessity, investigating judges may perform duties as judges at other specialized courts as appointed by the President of the First Instance Court.
