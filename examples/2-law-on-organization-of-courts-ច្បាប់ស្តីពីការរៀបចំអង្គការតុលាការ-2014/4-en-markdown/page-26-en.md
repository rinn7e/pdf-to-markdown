**Article 60.- (មាត្រា ៦០.-)**
The President of the Supreme Court takes necessary management measures for the operation of the Supreme Court and instructs all chambers of the Supreme Court to implement the law.

The President of the Supreme Court shall issue a decision (ដីកាសម្រេច) to:
- Appoint any Vice-president of the Supreme Court to manage the work on their behalf in case of necessity.
- Appoint a Vice-president of the Supreme Court to be in charge as the president of each chamber according to skills and work experience. In case of a lack of Vice-presidents, the President of the Supreme Court may appoint any judge to be the president of a chamber.

### Sub-section 2 (អនុផ្នែកទី ២)
### Jurisdiction (សមត្ថកិច្ច)

**Article 61.- (មាត្រា ៦១.-)**
The Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to resolve cases within its jurisdiction in accordance with the provisions of the law.

**Article 62.- (មាត្រា ៦២.-)**
A plenary chamber (សភាពេញអង្គ) shall be established to operate by a decision of the President of the Supreme Court to adjudicate, inter alia:
- On second appeals (បណ្តឹងសាទុក្ខលើកទីពីរ) against verdicts (សាលដីកា) of the Appeal Court, in accordance with the procedures in force.
- On motions for retrial (បណ្តឹងសើរើ) against final judgments (សាលក្រម) and verdicts, in accordance with the procedures in force.

The plenary chamber may also be established to operate to adjudicate on retrial motions or motions for re-adjudication in case there is a request for review and re-decision on any case from the King who is the President of the Supreme Council of Magistracy (ឧត្តមក្រុមប្រឹក្សានៃអង្គចៅក្រម) through the Minister of Justice.

The plenary chamber operates upon the invitation of the President of the Supreme Court.

The plenary chamber of the Supreme Court is the adjudicating body that issues decisions in the name of the Supreme Court.

When a plenary chamber is established, this chamber must have a composition of at least 09 (nine) judges who have judges representing all chambers of the Supreme Court.

The plenary chamber is under the presidency of the President of the Supreme Court.
