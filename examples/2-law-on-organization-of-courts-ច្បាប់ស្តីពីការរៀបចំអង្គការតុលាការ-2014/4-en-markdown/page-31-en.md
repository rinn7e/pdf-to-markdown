# Chapter 5 (ជំពូកទី ៥)
# Incompatibility (វិសមិតភាព)

**Article 74.- (មាត្រា ៧៤.-)**
The functions of prosecution, investigation, and adjudication must be separated from each other. Any judge who has already been in charge of handling any case in the capacity of a representative of the prosecution or as an investigating judge cannot adjudicate or participate in adjudicating that case which they handled; otherwise, the judgment or verdict shall be null and void.

**Article 75.- (មាត្រា ៧៥.-)**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating the same case together with one or more judges who are spouses (សហព័ទ្ធ) or blood relatives (ញាតិលោហិត) up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage (ញាតិពន្ធ) up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree, whether in the capacity of an adjudicating judge or a representative of the prosecution. This principle shall also be applied to judges in higher courts for cases adjudicated by judges in lower courts who are spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree.

**Article 76.- (មាត្រា ៧៦.-)**
All judges who have spouses or blood relatives up to the 4th (fourth) degree, including this 4th (fourth) degree, or relatives by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree, serving as a lawyer for any party in any case cannot participate in adjudicating that case.

**Article 77.- (មាត្រា ៧៧.-)**
A judge cannot participate in adjudicating any case where one party is a spouse or blood relative up to the 6th (sixth) degree, including this 6th (sixth) degree, or relative by marriage up to the 3rd (third) degree, including this 3rd (third) degree.
