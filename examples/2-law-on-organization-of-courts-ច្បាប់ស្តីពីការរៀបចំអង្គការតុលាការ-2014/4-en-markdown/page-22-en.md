**Article 50.- (មាត្រា ៥០.-)**
The functions of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court are entrusted to the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court. Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Appeal Court participate in implementing these functions under the leadership and collective responsibility of the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court.

In case of a task, illness, or absence, the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court shall appoint one Deputy Prosecutor General, or if there is no Deputy Prosecutor General, may appoint any Prosecutor to substitute.

In case the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court is in a situation of being unable to work, or in case the position of the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court is vacant, the Minister of Justice shall appoint any Deputy Prosecutor General to serve as the Acting Prosecutor General (អគ្គព្រះរាជអាជ្ញាស្តីទី) instead to ensure the operation of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Appeal Court until the official appointment of the Prosecutor General.

**Article 51.- (មាត្រា ៥១.-)**
The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court has management power over Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Appeal Court.

The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court appoints and allocates work to Deputy Prosecutors General and Prosecutors attached to the Appeal Court in various tasks of the General Prosecution Office and the trial chambers.

The conclusions (សេចក្តីសន្នីដ្ឋាន) of the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Appeal Court must be sent to the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court for review before the hearing. If the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court does not agree with that conclusion, and the representative of the General Prosecution Office who makes that conclusion still refuses to comply, the Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court may appoint another Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor or appoint themselves to be the representative of the General Prosecution Office in the hearing.

However, during the hearing, the Deputy Prosecutor General or Prosecutor attached to the Appeal Court may freely make oral observations according to what they deem to be in accordance with their conscience. No disciplinary prosecution can be made against the representative of the General Prosecution Office because of their oral observations during the hearing being different from the written conclusions.

The Prosecutor General attached to the Appeal Court has the right to issue orders (អធិបញ្ជា) to Prosecutors attached to the First Instance Court to prosecute, by implementing the provisions on criminal procedure in force.
