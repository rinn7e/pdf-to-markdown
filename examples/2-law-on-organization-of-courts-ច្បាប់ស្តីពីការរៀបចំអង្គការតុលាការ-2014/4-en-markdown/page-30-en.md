The General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court is divided into departments led by one head and several deputy heads as necessary.

The organization and functioning of the General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court shall be defined by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice.

**Article 72.- (មាត្រា ៧២.-)**
The General Secretariat of Administration of the Supreme Court is led by one Secretary General and several Deputy Secretaries General as necessary.

The Secretary General shall be appointed by Royal Decree upon request from the Prime Minister. The Minister of Justice consults with the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court to submit a request to the Prime Minister. The Secretary General shall be selected from civil servants who have at least 07 (seven) years of work experience in legal, administrative, or financial specializations.

Deputy Secretaries General and Heads of Departments shall be appointed by Sub-decree upon request from the Minister of Justice after consulting with the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court. Deputy Secretaries General and Heads of Departments shall be selected from civil servants who have at least 05 (five) years of work experience in legal, administrative, or financial specializations.

The officials working at the General Secretariat below the rank of Head of Department shall be appointed by Prokas (ប្រកាស) of the Minister of Justice. Deputy Heads of Departments as well as Heads and Deputy Heads of offices shall be selected from civil servants who have at least 03 (three) years of work experience in legal, administrative, or financial specializations.

All officials working at the General Secretariat, including the Secretary General, must have their framework at the Ministry of Justice.

**Article 73.- (មាត្រា ៧៣.-)**
The general assembly (សន្និបាត) of the Supreme Court shall take place under the presidency of the President of the Supreme Court and the Prosecutor General of the General Prosecution Office attached to the Supreme Court to summarize past work results and define the administrative work direction of the Supreme Court for the following year. The general assembly report of the Supreme Court shall be sent to the Ministry of Justice.
