**Article 37.- (មាត្រា ៣៧.-)**
An Appeal Court has specialized chambers (សភាជំនាញ) as follows:
- Criminal Chamber (សភាព្រហ្មទណ្ឌ)
- Civil Chamber (សភារដ្ឋប្បវេណី)
- Investigation Chamber (សភាស៊ើបសួរ)
- Commercial Chamber (សភាពាណិជ្ជកម្ម)
- Labour Chamber (សភាការងារ).

In case of necessity, other specialized chambers of the Appeal Court may be established by Royal Decree.

Each chamber issues its decisions autonomously within its jurisdiction in the name of the Appeal Court where the chamber is located.

**Article 38.- (មាត្រា ៣៨.-)**
The composition of each chamber includes:
- President of the Chamber (ប្រធានសភា)
- Judge (ចៅក្រម) and
- Court clerk (ក្រឡាបញ្ជី).

The president of each chamber shall be appointed by the President of the Appeal Court. The number of judges, court clerks, and the process of these chambers shall be defined by the President of the Appeal Court.

**Article 39.- (មាត្រា ៣៩.-)**
Each chamber of the Appeal Court adjudicates with 03 (three) judges, one of whom is the president, in accordance with the provisions on procedures in force.

In any case which the Supreme Court has remanded (បង្វិលមកវិញ), the Appeal Court shall adjudicate in a hearing which consists of 05 (five) judges, all of whom are judges who did not participate in adjudicating that case before, by implementing the provisions on procedures in force.
